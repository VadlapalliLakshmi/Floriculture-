History

Floriculture has ancient roots, originating in civilizations that valued ornamental plants. Over centuries, it evolved through European gardens, Victorian glasshouses, and modern greenhouse technology.
1. Ancient civilizations cultivated ornamental plants.Medieval Europe saw monastery gardens with medicinal and ornamental flowers.

Challenges to Floriculture:

loriculture faces several challenges that impact its sustainability and profitability. Issues such as climate change, which affects growing conditions and can lead to unpredictable weather patterns, pose a significant threat. Additionally, pests and diseases can devastate crops, requiring constant vigilance and often leading to the use of pesticides, which can have environmental and health consequences. Market volatility and competition, both locally and globally, also challenge floriculture businesses, requiring adaptability and strategic planning to remain profitable. Lastly, logistical challenges, including transportation costs and infrastructure limitations, can affect the timely delivery and quality of flowers to markets, further complicating the industry's operations. Addressing these challenges requires innovation, sustainability practices, and a deep understanding of both market dynamics and agricultural science.
